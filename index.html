Demo Visualizations for Tracking Keywords in American Studies
    Keywords Line Chart

The Keywords Line Chart allows you to pick out the individual keywords, as listed in Keywords in American Studies, and see how they often they represeted across the extent of the American Quarterly.

The chart will first show you all of the keywords at once, but if you click on an individual keyword in the legend at the bottom of the page, it will display only that word. After that, other keywords can be added to the display by clicking them once.

Moving your coursor on the display will bring up the year and the number of times the keyword appears in that year. Between the display and the list of keywords is a scroll bar sets the range of years on the display.

The chart is being displayed using Plot.ly javascript and includes Plotly tools in the top right hand corner that allow for zooming, changing the labels and exporting an image of the chart you have created on the display.

    Other Viz ideas
    Many Line Charts

The "Many Charts" graph recontextualized the data in the Interactive Line Chart. Here each keyword has it's own chart, and scaled to itself (with the highest peak of each chart being the point where the keyword appeared the most) allowing to the user to see the general trend of the keyword across time. This view makes it more clear that the general usage of keywords has increased over time, showing how the American Quarterly has steadily included a more diverse range of topics.

This view is a quantified approach to support arguments and anaylsis by XXXXX that the American Studies field has been steadily been including a wider range of topics.
